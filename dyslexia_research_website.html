Diagnosing Dyslexia in Children via Eye-Tracking

Revolutionary AI-powered approach using Operational Neural Networks to detect dyslexia through eye movement analysis

Explore Research

The Problem

Dyslexia affects 3-7% of the population, but current diagnostic methods are time-consuming, subjective, and often inaccessible.

Current Challenges

Traditional dyslexia diagnosis relies on extensive psychoeducational assessments that can take weeks or months to complete and require specialized experts.

Early Detection Critical

Early identification is crucial as undiagnosed dyslexia can negatively impact academic performance, self-esteem, and long-term educational outcomes.

Objective Measurement

Eye-tracking technology provides an objective window into reading behavior, capturing distinctive patterns that characterize dyslexic reading.

Our Approach

Eye-Tracking & AI Integration

We developed a novel dyslexia detection system using high-precision eye-tracking data processed through Operational Neural Networks (ONNs), which offer superior flexibility compared to traditional CNNs.

  • 163 participants: 2nd and 3rd grade children
  • High-resolution tracking: ≥120 Hz sampling rate
  • Dual reading tasks: Didactic and narrative texts
  • 9 key features: Spatiotemporal and efficiency metrics
  • Advanced AI: Operational Neural Networks

Key Findings

Distinctive Eye Movement Patterns: Children with dyslexia showed longer fixations (200-300ms+), more frequent regressions (backward eye movements), and shorter saccades compared to typical readers.


Feature Importance: The most predictive features included total reading time, gaze deviation patterns, regression count, and saccade characteristics - all capturing the increased cognitive effort in dyslexic reading.
